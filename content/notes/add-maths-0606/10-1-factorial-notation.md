## Core idea

Factorial notation, denoted by n!, is a mathematical shorthand representing the product of all positive integers from 1 up to n. It is the primary tool used to calculate the total number of ways a set of distinct items can be ordered in a single line without any restrictions.

## Key formulas

$n! = n \times (n-1) \times \cdots \times 1$, $0! = 1$

## Steps / method

Identify the total number of items (n) to be arranged.

Calculate the product: Multiply every integer from n down to 1.

Simplify fractions: When dividing factorials (e.g., n!/(n−r)!), cancel out common factors before multiplying to avoid excessively large numbers.

## Worked example — Basic Calculation

Question: Calculate the number of ways 6 different books can be arranged on a shelf.

Since all 6 books are different and all are being used, the number of ways is 6!. 6!=6×5×4×3×2×1=720.

## Worked example — Fractional Factorials

Question: Simplify 8!×2! 10! .

Expand the larger factorial until it matches the smaller one: 8!×2×110×9×8! . Cancel 8!: 210×9=45.

## Examiner tip

The 0! Exception: Many students lose marks in algebraic proofs by assuming 0!=0. Remember that 0!=1. This is essential for the formulas in Topic 10.3 and 10.4 to function correctly when n=r.

## Quick check

If n=4, then n!=24.
