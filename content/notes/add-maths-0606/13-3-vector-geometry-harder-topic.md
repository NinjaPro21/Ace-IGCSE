## Core idea

Vector geometry uses parallel vectors and scalar multiples to prove collinearity, find dividing points, and solve path problems. If $\vec{AB} = k\vec{BC}$, then $A$, $B$, and $C$ are collinear.

## Key formulas

$$
\mathbf{a} \parallel \mathbf{b} \iff \mathbf{a} = k\mathbf{b} \quad (k \neq 0)

\text{Point dividing } AB \text{ in ratio } m:n: \mathbf{p} = \frac{n\mathbf{a} + m\mathbf{b}}{m + n}
$$


## Steps / method

Express unknown vectors in terms of two base vectors (often $\mathbf{a}$ and $\mathbf{b}$).

Find two different routes to the same point and equate expressions.

Compare coefficients to solve for unknown scalars.

To prove collinearity, show one vector is a scalar multiple of another.

### Key rule

If $\vec{AB} = k\vec{BC}$, points $A$, $B$, and $C$ lie on a straight line.

## Worked example — Collinearity

$\vec{OA} = \mathbf{a}$, $\vec{OB} = \mathbf{b}$, and $\vec{OC} = 3\mathbf{a} - 2\mathbf{b}$. Show $A$, $B$, $C$ are collinear.

$$
\vec{AB} = \mathbf{b} - \mathbf{a}, \quad \vec{AC} = 3\mathbf{a} - 2\mathbf{b} - \mathbf{a} = 2\mathbf{a} - 2\mathbf{b} = -2(\mathbf{b} - \mathbf{a}) = -2\vec{AB}
$$
Since $\vec{AC} = -2\vec{AB}$, the vectors are parallel and the points are collinear.



## Worked example — Ratio division

Find the position vector of $P$ on $AB$ such that $AP : PB = 1 : 2$.

$$
\mathbf{p} = \frac{2\mathbf{a} + \mathbf{b}}{3}
$$

## Examiner tip

When equating vector expressions, compare $\mathbf{i}$ and $\mathbf{j}$ coefficients separately — do not mix components.

## Quick check

Collinear points share a common direction vector along the line.
