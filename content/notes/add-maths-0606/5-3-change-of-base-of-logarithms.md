## Core idea

Since most calculators only have buttons for "log" (base 10) and "ln" (base $e$), we need a way to convert logs of other bases into these standard forms. This formula allows you to change a log in base $a$ and $c$.

## Key formulas

$\log_a b = \frac{\log_c b}{\log_c a}$ $\log_a b = \frac{1}{\log_b a}$

## Steps / method

Choose a new base: Usually base 10 or base $e$ for calculation.

Apply the fraction: Put the "big number" ($b$ and $a$) in the bottom log.

Evaluate: Use your calculator to find the final decimal value.

## Worked example — IGCSE Style

Question: Evaluate $\log_5 20$ correct to 3 significant figures. Use base 10: $\frac{\log_{10} 20}{\log_{10} 5}$.

Calculate: $\frac{1.3010\ldots}{0.6989\ldots} \approx 1.86$. Answer: 1.86 (3 s.f.).

## Examiner tip

This formula is al so vital for solving equations where different bases are present, such as $\log_2 x = \log_4 (x+6)$. You must convert both to the same base (usually the smaller one) before you can solve.

## Quick check

$\log_2 3 \times \log_3 2 = 1$.
