## Core idea

Every straight line can be defined by an equation relating $x$ and $y$. The gradient-intercept form $y = mx + c$ highlights the slope and where the line crosses the $y$-axis.

## Key formulas

**Gradient–intercept form**

$$
y = mx + c
$$

**Point–gradient form**

$$
y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)
$$

## Steps / method

1. **Find the gradient** $m$ from two points or a parallel/perpendicular relationship.
2. **Substitute** a known point $(x_1, y_1)$ into $y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)$.
3. **Rearrange** to the required format ($y = mx + c$ or $ax + by + d = 0$).

### Key rule

Read the question carefully for the required form. If asked for $ax + by + c = 0$ with integer coefficients, do not leave the answer as $y = 0.5x + 2$.

## Worked example — Parallel line

Question: Find the equation of the line through $(4, -1)$ that is parallel to $y = 3x + 5$.

Since the lines are parallel, $m = 3$.

$$
y - (-1) = 3(x - 4)
$$

$$
y + 1 = 3x - 12 \Rightarrow y = 3x - 13
$$

## Examiner tip

If the examiner asks for $ax + by + c = 0$ where $a$, $b$, $c$ are integers, convert your final answer — leaving it as $y = mx + c$ loses the accuracy mark.

## Quick check

A vertical line has equation $x = k$ and its gradient is undefined.
