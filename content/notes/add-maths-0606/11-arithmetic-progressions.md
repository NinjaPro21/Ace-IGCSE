## Core idea

An arithmetic progression (AP) is a sequence of numbers where each term is found by adding a constant value, known as the common difference ($d$), to the preceding term. Problems typically involve finding a specific term in the sequence or calculating the sum of the first $n$ terms.

## Key formulas

$$
\text{nth Term: } u_n = a + (n - 1)d
$$
$$
\text{Sum of first n terms: } S_n = \frac{n}{2}[2a + (n - 1)d] \quad \text{or} \quad S_n = \frac{n}{2}[a + l]
$$
$$
\text{Where: } a = \text{first term}, \,\, d = \text{common difference}, \,\, l = \text{last term}
$$

## Steps / method

Extract the value of the first term ($a$) directly from the given sequence.

Find the common difference ($d$) by subtracting any term from the one that follows it ($u_2 - u_1$).

To find a specific term, substitute $a$, $d$, and the term number $n$ into the formula $u_n = a + (n-1)d$.

To find the sum of the terms, choose the appropriate $S_n$ formula based on whether the final term $l$ is known.

If $a$ and $d$ are unknown, use the information provided to set up and solve a system of simultaneous linear equations.

### Key rule

The common difference $d$ is constant throughout an AP. Use $u_n = a + (n-1)d$ for a single term and $S_n$ for a sum — never interchange them.

## Worked example — Finding a term

The AP $5, 8, 11, 14, \ldots$ has $a = 5$ and $d = 3$. Find the 20th term.

$$
u_{20} = 5 + (20 - 1) \times 3 = 5 + 57 = 62
$$
The 20th term is **62**.

## Worked example — Sum of terms

Find the sum of the first 10 terms of the AP with $a = 4$ and $d = 2$.

$$
S_{10} = \frac{10}{2}[2(4) + (10 - 1)(2)] = 5[8 + 18] = 130
$$
The sum is **130**.

## Examiner tip

Be careful not to confuse the formula for the $n$-th term ($u_n$) with the formula for the sum of $n$ terms ($S_n$). Giving the sum when asked for a specific term is a frequent error that leads to a loss of marks.

## Quick check

If the common difference $d$ in an arithmetic sequence is negative, the terms will decrease in value as $n$ increases.
