## Core idea

Definite integration evaluates an integral between two specified numerical boundaries, removing the constant of integration ($C$) to find a single numerical value.

## Key formulas

$$
\int_{a}^{b} f(x) \, dx = [F(x)]_{a}^{b} = F(b) - F(a)
$$

## Steps / method

Integrate the given function, omitting $+C$, and enclose the result in square brackets with limits.

Substitute the upper limit $b$, then the lower limit $a$.

Subtract: $F(b) - F(a)$.

### Key rule

Evaluate $\int_a^b f(x)\, dx$ as $F(b) - F(a)$ with no constant $C$.

## Worked example — Basic definite integral

Evaluate $\int_1^3 (2x + 1)\, dx$.

$$
\int_1^3 (2x + 1)\, dx = \left[x^2 + x\right]_1^3 = (9 + 3) - (1 + 1) = 10
$$

## Worked example — Evaluate with substitution

Evaluate $\int_0^2 (3x^2 - 2)\, dx$.

$$
\left[x^3 - 2x\right]_0^2 = (8 - 4) - 0 = 4
$$

## Examiner tip

Show the bracket notation $[F(x)]_a^b$ before substituting limits — examiners award method marks for this step.

## Quick check

$\int_0^1 2x\, dx = [x^2]_0^1 = 1$.
