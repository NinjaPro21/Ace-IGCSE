## Core idea

When $f(x)$ is quadratic, the graph of $y = |f(x)|$ is found by sketching the parent parabola first, then reflecting any part below the $x$-axis upward. The result is always on or above the $x$-axis ($y \ge 0$).

## Key formulas

**Modulus of a quadratic**

$$
y = |f(x)| \quad \Rightarrow \quad y \ge 0 \text{ always}
$$

**Reflection rule**

$$
\text{Where } f(x) \ge 0 \text{: graph unchanged}
$$

$$
\text{Where } f(x) < 0 \text{: reflect upward}
$$

**Vertex after reflection**

If the parent vertex is $(h, k)$ with $k < 0$, the new peak is $(h, |k|)$.

## Steps / method

1. Sketch the parent quadratic $y = f(x)$ lightly (dotted line).
2. Mark the roots where $f(x) = 0$.
3. Reflect any section below the $x$-axis upward.
4. Label the roots and any reflected peaks.

### Key rule

If $f(x) \ge 0$ for all $x (e.g$(2 $), then$|f(x)| = f(x)$and the graph is unchanged.$

## Worked example — W shape

Question: Sketch $y = |x^2 - 4x + 3|$.

Factor the parent: $x^2 - 4x + 3 = (x-1)(x-3)$, so roots are $x = 1$ and $x = 3$.

Vertex at $x = 2$:

$$
y = 4 - 8 + 3 = -1
$$

The parent vertex is $(2, -1)$, below the axis. Reflect the dip between $x = 1$ and $x = 3$ upward to get a bump at $(2, 1)$. Outside the roots the graph matches the parent smile — a **W-shape**.

## Worked example — Counting solutions

Question: How many solutions does $|x^2 - 2| = 1$ have?

Sketch $y = |x^2 - 2|$. Parent roots at $\pm\sqrt{2}$; vertex $(0, -2)$ reflects to $(0, 2)$.

The horizontal line $y = 1$ cuts the W-shape **4 times**, so there are **4 solutions**.

## Examiner tip

Always sketch the parent parabola first, then only flip the negative parts. Label roots and the reflected peak. Do not give the y-intercept as $c$ — for $y = |ax^2 + bx + c|$ it is $|c|$.

## Quick check

For $y = |x^2 + 1|$, does the graph change from $y = x^2 + 1$? No — because $x^2 + 1 \ge 0$ for all real $x$.
