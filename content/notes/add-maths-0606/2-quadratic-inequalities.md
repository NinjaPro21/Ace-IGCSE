## Core idea

Quadratic inequalities ask for the range of $x$-values where a quadratic curve sits either strictly above ($&gt; 0$) or below ($&lt; 0$) the horizontal x-axis. Solving them requires finding the critical boundary roots first, followed by visual inspections of a sketch to determine the intervals.

## Key formulas

$$ax^2 + bx + c &gt; 0 \quad \text{or} \quad ax^2 + bx + c &lt; 0$$

$$\text{Critical values: } x_1, x_2 \quad (\text{where } x_1 &lt; x_2)$$

## Steps / method

Rearrange the entire inequality so that all terms are on one side, leaving zero on the opposite side.

Temporarily replace the inequality sign with an equals sign and solve $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$ to find critical root boundaries.

Sketch a quick, rough diagram of the parabola based on whether $a$ is positive (smiley) or negative (frowning).

For $f(x) &gt; 0$ (with $a &gt; 0$), choose the outer regions: $x &lt; x_1$ or $x &gt; x_2$.

For $f(x) &lt; 0$ (with $a &gt; 0$), choose the inner bounded region: $x_1 &lt; x &lt; x_2$.

## Examiner tip

Never write two separate outer inequalities as a single joined chain like $5 &lt; x &lt; 2$. Write separate individual inequalities using the word &quot;or&quot; ($x &lt; 2 \text{ or } x &gt; 5$) to maintain logical and mathematical correctness.

## Quick check

If $a &gt; 0$ and the inequality asks for $f(x) &lt; 0$, then the final solution is always a single combined range between the two roots.
