## Core idea

Trigonometric graphs are periodic waves. The modulus function $|f(x)|$ reflects any part of the curve below the x-axis upward, so all $y$-values are non-negative.

## Key formulas

**Sine and cosine graph**

$y = a\sin(bx) + c$ has amplitude $|a|$ and period $\frac{360°}{|b|}$ (degrees) or $\frac{2\pi}{|b|}$ (radians).

**Modulus graph**

$y = |f(x)|$ — replace negative values of $f(x)$ by their opposites.

## Steps / method

1. **Identify** amplitude and period from the equation.

2. **Sketch** the parent curve (intercepts and turning points).

3. **Apply modulus** — reflect any negative section above the x-axis.

4. **Label** all points where the graph meets the axes.

### Key rule

For $y = \tan x$, draw vertical asymptotes at $90°$ and $270°$. The curve must not cross these lines.

## Worked example — Modulus sketch

Question: Sketch $y = |\cos x|$ for $0° \le x \le 360°$.

$y = \cos x$ starts at $(0, 1)$, crosses zero at $90°$, reaches $-1$ at $180°$, crosses zero at $270°$, and returns to $1$ at $360°$.

Between $90°$ and $270°$, $\cos x < 0$. Reflect this part upward: $y = |\cos x|$ has peaks at $0°$, $180°$, and $360°$.

## Worked example — Amplitude and period

Question: State the amplitude and period of $y = 4\sin(3x)$.

Amplitude $= 4$. Period $= \frac{360°}{3} = 120°$.

## Examiner tip

When sketching $y = \tan x$, show vertical asymptotes at $90°$ and $270°$. If your curve touches these lines, you will lose marks for technical inaccuracy.

## Quick check

The range of $y = |3\sin x|$ is $0 \le y \le 3$.
