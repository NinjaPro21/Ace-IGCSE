## Core idea

Solving a cubic equation $ax^3 + bx^2 + cx + d = 0$ usually means finding one linear factor first, then dividing to get a quadratic that can be factorised or solved with the formula.

## Key formulas

**Factorised cubic**

$$
ax^3 + bx^2 + cx + d = (x - a)(Ax^2 + Bx + C)
$$

## Steps / method

1. **Find one root** — use the Factor Theorem (trial and error) to find $f(a) = 0$.
2. **Reduce to quadratic** — divide the cubic by $(x - a)$ to get the quadratic quotient.
3. **Factor the quadratic** — factorise or use the quadratic formula.
4. **List all roots** — a cubic has up to 3 real roots.

### Key rule

If the quadratic part does not factorise neatly, use the quadratic formula for the remaining roots.

## Worked example — Full solution

Question: Solve $x^3 - 6x^2 + 11x - 6 = 0$.

Trial $x = 1$: $1 - 6 + 11 - 6 = 0$, so $(x - 1)$ is a factor.

Divide to get $x^2 - 5x + 6 = (x - 2)(x - 3)$.

The roots are $x = 1, 2, 3$.

## Worked example — Repeated roots

Question: Solve $x^3 - 3x + 2 = 0$.

$f(1) = 0$, so $(x - 1)$ is a factor.

Division gives $(x - 1)(x^2 + x - 2) = (x - 1)^2(x + 2)$.

Roots: $x = 1$ (repeated) and $x = -2$.

## Examiner tip

If the quadratic part does not factorise nicely, use the quadratic formula to find the remaining roots, even if they involve surds.

## Quick check

A cubic graph must cross the $x$-axis at least once.
