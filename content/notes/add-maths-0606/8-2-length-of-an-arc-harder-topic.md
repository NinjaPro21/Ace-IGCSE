## Core idea

Arc length $s$ is the distance along the curved edge of a sector. In radians, the formula $s = r\theta$ is a simple linear relationship — no conversion factor needed.

## Key formulas

**Arc length**

$$
s = r\theta \quad (\theta \text{ in radians})
$$

**Perimeter of a sector**

$$
P = 2r + s = 2r + r\theta
$$

## Steps / method

1. **Check the angle** — convert to radians if given in degrees.
2. **Substitute** $r$ and $\theta$ into $s = r\theta$.
3. **Solve** for the unknown if arc length, radius, or angle is missing.
4. **For perimeter**, add the two radii: $P = 2r + s$.

### Key rule

"Perimeter" of a sector means the arc **plus both radii** — not just the arc length.

## Worked example — Arc length

Question: A circle has radius $8$ cm. Find the arc length for an angle of $1.2$ rad.

$$
s = r\theta = 8 \times 1.2 = 9.6 \text{ cm}
$$

## Worked example — Perimeter

Question: A sector has radius $5$ cm and angle $\frac{\pi}{4}$ rad. Find its perimeter.

$s = 5 \times \frac{\pi}{4} = \frac{5\pi}{4} \approx 3.93$ cm

$P = 5 + 5 + 3.93 = 13.9$ cm (3 s.f.)

## Examiner tip

When a question asks for the perimeter, include the arc length **and** the two straight radii. Giving only the arc loses marks.

## Quick check

When $\theta = 2\pi$, the formula gives $s = r(2\pi) = 2\pi r$, the full circumference.
