## Core idea

This foundational topic applies the ratios of sine, cosine, and tangent to right-angled triangles. It is used to calculate unknown lengths and angles when the geometry is restricted to acute angles, serving as the starting point for all circular functions.

## Key formulas

$\sin\theta = \frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Hypotenuse}}$

$\cos\theta = \frac{\text{Adjacent}}{\text{Hypotenuse}}$

$\tan\theta = \frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Adjacent}}$

$\tan\theta = \frac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta}$

## Steps / method

Label the Triangle: Identify the Hypotenuse (longest side), Opposite (across from θ), and Adjacent (next to θ).

Select the Ratio: Use the mnemonic SOH CAH TOA to determine which formula fits your knowns and unknowns.

Solve for Sides: Multiply or divide the ratio by the known side.

Solve for Angles: Use the inverse functions ($\sin^{-1}$ , $\cos^{-1}$ , $\tan^{-1}$) on your calculator.

## Examiner tip

Degree Mode vs. Radian Mode: Students frequently lose marks by leaving their calculators in Radian mode (from Chapter 8) when solving Chapter 9 problems. Always verify the "D" or "DEG" icon is visible on your screen before performing calculations in degrees.

## Quick check

If $\theta=45^{\circ}$ , then $\tan\theta=1$ because the opposite and adjacent sides are equal.
