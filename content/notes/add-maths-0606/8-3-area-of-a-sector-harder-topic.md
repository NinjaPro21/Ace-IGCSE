## Core idea

The area of a sector is the region enclosed by two radii and an arc. In radians, the area is $A = \frac{1}{2}r^2\theta$ — directly proportional to the square of the radius and the angle.

## Key formulas

**Sector area**

$$
A = \frac{1}{2}r^2\theta \quad (\theta \text{ in radians})
$$

**Segment area**

$$
A_{\text{segment}} = \frac{1}{2}r^2(\theta - \sin\theta)
$$

## Steps / method

1. **Identify** $r$ and $\theta$ — ensure $\theta$ is in radians.
2. **Calculate sector area** using $A = \frac{1}{2}r^2\theta$.
3. **For a segment** (between chord and arc), subtract the triangle area $\frac{1}{2}r^2\sin\theta$ from the sector area.

### Key rule

When finding $\sin\theta$ for a segment, your calculator must be in **radian** mode if $\theta$ is in radians.

## Worked example — Sector area

Question: Find the area of a sector with radius $6$ cm and angle $\frac{2\pi}{3}$ rad.

$$
A = \frac{1}{2} \times 6^2 \times \frac{2\pi}{3} = \frac{1}{2} \times 36 \times \frac{2\pi}{3} = 12\pi \approx 37.7 \text{ cm}^2
$$

## Worked example — Segment area

Question: A sector has radius $10$ cm and angle $1$ rad. Find the segment area.

Sector: $A = \frac{1}{2} \times 10^2 \times 1 = 50$ cm²

Triangle: $\frac{1}{2} \times 10^2 \times \sin(1) \approx 41.9$ cm²

Segment: $50 - 41.9 = 8.1$ cm² (3 s.f.)

## Examiner tip

For segment problems, use radian mode on your calculator when evaluating $\sin\theta$ with $\theta$ in radians.

## Quick check

When $\theta = 2\pi$, the formula gives $A = \frac{1}{2}r^2(2\pi) = \pi r^2$, the full circle area.
