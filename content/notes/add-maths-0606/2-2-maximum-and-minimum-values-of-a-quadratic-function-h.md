## Core idea

Every parabola has a turning point (vertex) which represents the maximum or minimum value of the function. We use **completing the square** to transform the equation into a form where this vertex is immediately visible.

## Key formulas

**Completed square form**

$$
y = a(x - h)^2 + k
$$

**Vertex coordinates**

$$
(h, k)
$$

**Nature of turning point**

- If $a > 0$ (smile shape): **minimum** value is $k$ at $x = h$
- If $a < 0$ (frown shape): **maximum** value is $k$ at $x = h$

## Steps / method

1. **Factor out** the coefficient of $x^2$ from the $x^2$ and $x$ terms.

2. **Complete the square** inside the brackets by adding and subtracting $\left(\frac{b}{2a}\right)^2$.

3. **Simplify** into the form $a(x - h)^2 + k$.

4. **Identify** the vertex $(h, k)$ and state the maximum or minimum value.

### Key rule

Flip the sign for $h$ but keep the sign for $k$. If $a > 0$, you get a **minimum**; if $a < 0$, you get a **maximum**.

## Worked example — Finding the minimum

Question: Express $f(x) = 2x^2 - 12x + 7$ in the form $a(x - h)^2 + k$ and state the minimum value.

Factor out 2:

$$
f(x) = 2(x^2 - 6x) + 7
$$

Complete the square:

$$
f(x) = 2[(x - 3)^2 - 9] + 7 = 2(x - 3)^2 - 18 + 7 = 2(x - 3)^2 - 11
$$

The vertex is $(3, -11)$. The **minimum value** is $-11$ when $x = 3$.

## Worked example — Finding the maximum

Question: Find the maximum value of $g(x) = 5 + 4x - x^2$.

Rearrange and factor out $-1$:

$$
g(x) = -(x^2 - 4x) + 5
$$

Complete the square:

$$
g(x) = -[(x - 2)^2 - 4] + 5 = -(x - 2)^2 + 4 + 5 = -(x - 2)^2 + 9
$$

The **maximum value** is $9$ when $x = 2$.

## Examiner tip

The question often asks for the "coordinates of the stationary point." This is just the vertex $(h, k)$. Ensure you flip the sign for $h$ but keep the sign for $k$.

## Quick check

If $a > 0$, the parabola opens upward like a **smile** — you get a **minimum**. If $a < 0$, it opens downward like a **frown** — you get a **maximum**.
