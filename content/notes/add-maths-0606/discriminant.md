## What is the Discriminant?

For a quadratic $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$, the **discriminant** tells you the nature of the roots before you solve:

$$\Delta = b^2 - 4ac$$

## How to use it

1. Identify $a$, $b$, and $c$ from your equation.
2. Calculate $\Delta$.
3. Interpret the result using the nature-of-roots table.

## Line and curve intersections

When a line $y = mx + c$ meets a curve, substitute to form a quadratic in $x$. The discriminant of that quadratic tells you whether there are **two**, **one**, or **no** intersection points.
