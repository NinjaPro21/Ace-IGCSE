## Core idea

Circular measure uses **radians** — an angle measured by the arc length divided by the radius. One radian is the angle when the arc length equals the radius. Radians simplify calculus and circular measure formulas.

## Key formulas

**Degrees and radians**

$$
\pi \text{ rad} = 180°
$$

**Degrees to radians**

$$
\text{radians} = \text{degrees} \times \frac{\pi}{180}
$$

**Radians to degrees**

$$
\text{degrees} = \text{radians} \times \frac{180}{\pi}
$$

## Steps / method

1. **Identify the unit** — is the angle in degrees or radians?

2. **Apply the conversion factor** — multiply by $\frac{\pi}{180}$ or $\frac{180}{\pi}$.

3. **Simplify** — leave answers in terms of $\pi (e.g$ (2) or as a decimal (3 s.f.).

### Key rule

Always check your calculator is in **DEG** or **RAD** mode before evaluating trigonometric functions.

## Worked example — Converting angles

Question: Convert $135°$ to radians, and convert $2.5$ rad to degrees.

$135° \times \frac{\pi}{180} = \frac{3\pi}{4}$ rad

$2.5 \times \frac{180}{\pi} \approx 143.2°$

## Examiner tip

Wrong calculator mode is the most common failure in circular measure. Check **DEG** vs **RAD** before every trig or circular measure calculation.

## Quick check

$\frac{\pi}{2}$ rad equals $90°$ (a right angle).
