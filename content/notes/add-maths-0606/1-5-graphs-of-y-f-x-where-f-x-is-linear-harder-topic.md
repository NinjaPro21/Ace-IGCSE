## Core idea

To sketch $y = |mx + c|$, draw the line $y = mx + c$ and reflect any part below the $x$-axis upwards. This creates a **V-shape** with its vertex on the $x$-axis.

## Key formulas

**Vertex (x-intercept)**

$$
mx + c = 0 \Rightarrow x = -\frac{c}{m}
$$

**y-intercept**

$$
y\text{-intercept} = |c|
$$

## Steps / method

1. **Find the vertex** — solve $mx + c = 0$ to get the $x$-intercept where the graph touches the axis.
2. **Find the y-intercept** — substitute $x = 0$ and take the modulus: $|c|$.
3. **Sketch** — draw the V-shape through the vertex and y-intercept, with both arms going upward.

### Key rule

The range of $y = |f(x)|$ is always $y \ge 0$.

## Worked example — Sketching

Question: Sketch $y = |x - 4|$.

The vertex is at $x = 4$ (where $x - 4 = 0$).

The $y$-intercept is $|{-4}| = 4$.

The graph is a V-shape with vertex at $(4, 0)$ passing through $(0, 4)$.

## Worked example — Finding intersections

Question: Find the coordinates where $y = |2x - 6|$ intersects $y = 4$.

$$
|2x - 6| = 4
$$

**Case 1:** $2x - 6 = 4 \Rightarrow 2x = 10 \Rightarrow x = 5$

**Case 2:** $2x - 6 = -4 \Rightarrow 2x = 2 \Rightarrow x = 1$

The coordinates of intersection are $(1, 4)$ and $(5, 4)$.

## Examiner tip

When sketching, label the coordinates of the intercepts and the vertex to gain full marks.
