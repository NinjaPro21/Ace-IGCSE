## Core idea

The roots of a quadratic are the values of $x$ where the curve crosses the $x$-axis. We use the **discriminant** ($\Delta$) to predict how many roots exist without actually solving the equation.

## Key formulas

**Discriminant**

$$
\Delta = b^2 - 4ac
$$

**Nature of roots**

| Condition | Roots |
|-----------|-------|
| $\Delta > 0$ | 2 distinct real roots |
| $\Delta = 0$ | 1 repeated real root (tangent) |
| $\Delta < 0$ | No real roots |

**For real roots**

$$
b^2 - 4ac \ge 0
$$

## Steps / method

1. **Arrange** into $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$.

2. **Calculate** $b^2 - 4ac$.

3. **Interpret** based on the criteria above.

### Key rule

If a question says a line is a **tangent** to a curve, set the discriminant of the combined equation to **zero**.

## Worked example — Finding $k$

Question: Find the values of $k$ for which $x^2 + kx + 9 = 0$ has real roots.

For real roots, $b^2 - 4ac \ge 0$:

$$
k^2 - 4(1)(9) \ge 0 \Rightarrow k^2 \ge 36
$$

So $k \le -6$ or $k \ge 6$.

## Worked example — Nature of roots

Question: Describe the roots of $2x^2 + 3x + 5 = 0$.

$$
\Delta = 3^2 - 4(2)(5) = 9 - 40 = -31
$$

Since $\Delta < 0$, there are **no real roots**.

## Examiner tip

If a question says a line is a tangent to a curve, you must set the discriminant of the combined equation to zero.

## Quick check

If $b^2 - 4ac$ is a perfect square, the roots are rational.
