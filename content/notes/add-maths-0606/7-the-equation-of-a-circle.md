## Core idea

A circle is defined as the locus of all points that are a fixed distance (the radius) away from a central coordinate point. Expanding the standard radius equation gives the general form of a circle, which requires completing the square to find its center and radius.

## Key formulas

$$\text{Standard Form: } (x - h)^2 + (y - k)^2 = r^2 \quad (\text{Center: } (h,k), \text{ Radius: } r)$$

$$\text{General Form: } x^2 + y^2 + 2gx + 2fy + c = 0 \quad (\text{Center: } (-g, -f), \text{ Radius: } \sqrt{g^2 + f^2 - c})$$

## Steps / method

Group all $x$ terms together and all $y$ terms together, moving any plain constant values to the opposite side of the equation.

Complete the square for the $x$ terms group, and add the matching balancing squared constant to the right-hand side.

Complete the square for the $y$ terms group, and add its matching balancing squared constant to the right-hand side.

Factor both quadratic groups into their final perfect square forms: $(x - h)^2 + (y - k)^2 = \text{Constant}$.

Extract the center coordinates $(h, k)$ and take the square root of the right-hand constant to determine the radius $r$.

## Examiner tip

Watch out for the signs of the center coordinates. In the equation $(x + 3)^2 + (y - 4)^2 = 25$, the $x$-coordinate of the center is $-3$ and the $y$-coordinate is $+4$. Mixing up these signs is a frequent source of lost marks.

## Quick check

If the constant value on the right-hand side of a completed circle equation is negative or zero, no real physical circle can exist.
