## Core idea

The laws of logarithms are algebraic rules that allow you to combine, split, or simplify logarithmic expressions that share an identical base. Use these rules to condense complicated, multi-term logarithmic equations into a single logarithmic term before removing the operator to solve for the variable. These laws correspond directly to the standard laws of indices because logarithms are exponents.

## Steps / method

Scan the algebraic expression to ensure all component logarithmic terms share an identical base value before applying any laws.

Use the Power Law to move any numerical coefficients in front of a logarithm up to become exponents on their respective arguments.

Apply the Product Law to combine separate added logarithms into a single log term containing the product of their arguments.

Apply the Quotient Law to combine subtracted logarithms, placing the subtracted argument into the denominator of a single log expression.
