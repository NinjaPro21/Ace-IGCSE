## Core idea

For a cubic $f(x)$, the graph of $y = |f(x)|$ takes the S-shape of the cubic and reflects any negative loops above the $x$-axis. This often produces a W-shape or a series of peaks.

## Key formulas

**Modulus cubic**

$$
y = |k(x - a)(x - b)(x - c)|
$$

The roots remain at $x = a, b, c$.

## Steps / method

1. **Find intercepts** — solve $f(x) = 0$ for $x$-intercepts; evaluate $f(0)$ for the $y$-intercept.

2. **Sketch the parent cubic** — draw lightly, noting end behaviour.

3. **Apply the modulus** — reflect any part where $y < 0$ across the $x$-axis.

4. **Label peaks** — local minima of the parent often become local maxima after reflection.

### Key rule

For a repeated root like $(x - 1)^2$, the graph touches the axis at $x = 1$. After applying the modulus, it may become a local minimum on the axis.

## Worked example — Sketching

Question: Sketch $y = |x(x - 2)(x + 2)|$.

Roots are $x = -2, 0, 2$. The $y$-intercept is $0$.

The parent cubic goes through $(-2, 0)$, peaks, through $(0, 0)$, dips between $0$ and 2, then rises to the right.

Reflect the negative section between $0$ and 2 upward into a peak.

## Worked example — Counting solutions

Question: How many solutions does $|(x - 1)^2(x + 2)| = 1$ have?

The graph has a bounce root at $x = 1$ and a cross root at $x = -2$. The section left of $x = -2$ is reflected up.

The line $y = 1$ crosses the left reflected arm, the peak between $-2$ and $1$, and the right arm — **3 solutions**.

## Examiner tip

Be careful with repeated roots like $(x - 1)^2$. In a normal cubic this is a tangent point. In a modulus cubic it stays on the axis but may become a local minimum.

## Quick check

The range of $y = |f(x)|$ for any cubic is $y \ge 0$.
