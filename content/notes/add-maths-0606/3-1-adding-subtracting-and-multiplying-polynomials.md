## Core idea

Polynomials are algebraic expressions with multiple terms. We combine **like terms** when adding or subtracting, and use the **distributive law** when multiplying.

## Key formulas

**FOIL / expansion**

$$
(a + b)(c + d) = ac + ad + bc + bd
$$

**Degree of a product**

$$
\text{Degree of product} = \text{Degree of } P_1 + \text{Degree of } P_2
$$

## Steps / method

1. **Addition/Subtraction** — align like terms (same power of $x$) and add or subtract coefficients.

2. **Multiplication** — multiply every term in the first polynomial by every term in the second.

3. **Simplify** — combine like terms and write in descending powers of $x$.

### Key rule

When subtracting a polynomial in brackets, change the sign of **every** term inside.

## Worked example — Multiplying polynomials

Question: Simplify $(2x^2 - 3x + 4)(x - 2)$.

$$
\begin{aligned}
&2x^2(x - 2) = 2x^3 - 4x^2 \
&-3x(x - 2) = -3x^2 + 6x \
&4(x - 2) = 4x - 8
\end{aligned}
$$

Combine:

$$
2x^3 - 7x^2 + 10x - 8
$$

## Examiner tip

When subtracting a polynomial in brackets, e.g. $-(x^2 - 5x + 6)$, change every sign: $-x^2 + 5x - 6$. Students often forget to flip the sign of every term.

## Quick check

If you multiply a quadratic (degree 2) by a linear expression (degree 1), the result must be a cubic (degree 3).
