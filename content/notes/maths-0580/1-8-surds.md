## Core idea

Surds represent exact irrational values that cannot be written as terminating or recurring decimals, leaving them enclosed within square or cube root symbols. Rationalising a denominator transforms expressions containing fractional surds into a form with a rational denominator. Use surd laws to maintain absolute precision throughout multi-step geometric calculations without introducing rounding errors.

## Key formulas

**1 — Surd laws**

$$
\sqrt{ab} = \sqrt{a} \times \sqrt{b}, \quad \sqrt{\frac{a}{b}} = \frac{\sqrt{a}}{\sqrt{b}}, \quad \sqrt{a} \times \sqrt{a} = a
$$

**2 — Rationalising the denominator**

$$
\frac{c}{\sqrt{a}} = \frac{c \times \sqrt{a}}{\sqrt{a} \times \sqrt{a}} = \frac{c\sqrt{a}}{a}
$$

## Steps / method

Simplify a surd by finding its largest perfect square factor ($4, 9, 16, 25, 36, \dots$).

Split the root expression using the multiplication law, evaluate the perfect square root, and write the remaining non-square component under the root symbol.

Combine like surds through basic addition or subtraction in the same way you collect like terms in algebra.

To remove a radical from a denominator, multiply both the numerator and the denominator by that same root term or its conjugate expression.

## Examiner tip

Make sure you understand the difference between an exact value and a rounded decimal value on Paper 4. If a question asks for an answer "in its simplest surd form," providing a rounded decimal answer from your calculator will score zero marks. Leave your final answer expressed explicitly with its root symbols intact (e.g., $3\sqrt{5}$).

## Quick check

Multiplying two identical square root surds together removes the radical sign completely, leaving the underlying rational integer value.

## Worked example — Example 1 (June 2023 P22 Q19)

Simplify fully the surd expression: $\sqrt{75} - \sqrt{12}$.

Find the largest perfect square factor for each radical value. For $75$, it is $25$. For $12$, it is $4$.

Rewrite both surd terms using these perfect square factors: $\sqrt{25 \times 3} - \sqrt{4 \times 3}$.

Apply the multiplication law to split and simplify the roots: $\sqrt{25} \times \sqrt{3} - \sqrt{4} \times \sqrt{3} = 5\sqrt{3} - 2\sqrt{3}$.

Combine these matching surd terms by subtracting their coefficients: $(5 - 2)\sqrt{3} = 3\sqrt{3}$.

## Worked example — Example 2 (Nov 2022 P21 Q23)

Rationalise the denominator and simplify: $\frac{6}{\sqrt{3}}$.

Identify that the denominator contains a radical term ($\sqrt{3}$).

Multiply both the top and bottom of the fraction by $\sqrt{3}$ to remove the radical from the denominator without changing the value of the fraction: $\frac{6 \times \sqrt{3}}{\sqrt{3} \times \sqrt{3}}$.

Evaluate the multiplication steps: the numerator becomes $6\sqrt{3}$, and the denominator simplifies to $3$.

Construct the simplified fractional layout: $\frac{6\sqrt{3}}{3}$.

Cancel down the rational whole numbers ($6 \div 3 = 2$) to find the final answer: $2\sqrt{3}$.
