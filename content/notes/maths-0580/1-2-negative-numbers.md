## Core idea

Negative numbers define continuous values lying strictly below zero on a standard real number line. They govern operations involving directed quantities, such as temperature fluctuations, financial positions, and vector translations. Use these sign-interaction rules whenever you combine directed terms or clear signs during complex algebraic manipulations.

## Key formulas

$$
\text{Sign Product Rules:} \quad (+) \times (-) = -, \quad (-) \times (+) = -, \quad (-) \times (-) = +

\text{Double Sign Simplification:} \quad a - (-b) = a + b, \quad a + (-b) = a - b
$$

## Steps / method

Simplify adjacent double signs within the expression into a single operator using standard sign product mechanics.

When adding or subtracting directed values, locate the starting value on a conceptual number line and move right for positive additions or left for negative modifications.

For multiplication or division, compute the absolute numerical values first ignoring any attached symbols.

Apply the final sign to your product or quotient based on the number of negative signs counted during step three: an odd number yields a negative result, whereas an even number yields a positive result.

## Examiner tip

Be exceptionally careful when squaring a negative number on a calculator. Writing $-5^2$ without brackets commands the calculator to square $5$ first and then apply a negative sign, yielding $-25$. To square the negative integer itself, you must enclose it completely in brackets: $(-5)^2 = 25$. This distinction causes widespread errors in quadratic formula substitutions on Paper 4.

## Quick check

If you multiply or divide two negative values together, the final resulting value will always become positive.

## Worked example — Example 1 (June 2020 P2 Q2)

Simplify the numerical expression: $-8 - 3 \times (-4 + 1)$.

Resolve operations inside the brackets first: $-4 + 1 = -3$.

Rewrite the expression incorporating the simplified bracket value: $-8 - 3 \times (-3)$.

Perform the multiplication step, tracking signs carefully: $-3 \times (-3) = +9$. Combine the remaining terms: $-8 + 9$. Evaluate the final addition on the number line to get: $1$.

## Worked example — Example 2 (Nov 2022 P1 Q7)

Evaluate the expression $\frac{-20}{( -2 ) \times ( -3 ) - ( -1 )}$.

Isolate the denominator expression to evaluate it independently: $(-2) \times (-3) - (-1)$.

Calculate the multiplication within the denominator: $(-2) \times (-3) = 6$.

Substitute back and clear the double negative sign: $6 - (-1) = 6 + 1 = 7$.

Reconstruct the complete fractional layout: $\frac{-20}{7}$.

Convert to a mixed number if requested, keeping track of the overall single negative sign: $-2\frac{6}{7}$.
