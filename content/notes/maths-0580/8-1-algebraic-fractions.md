## Core idea

Algebraic fractions are fractions where the numerator, denominator, or both contain algebraic expressions. They are simplified, added, subtracted, multiplied, or divided using the same operational rules as numerical fractions. Use these techniques to simplify complex fractional expressions or to convert fractional equations into single-line linear or quadratic equations.

Algebraic fractions — factorise numerator and denominator, then cancel common factors.

## Steps / method

To simplify a single algebraic fraction, fully factorise the numerator and denominator expressions first.

Cancel out any identical bracketed factor groups that appear in both.

For multiplication, multiply the numerators together and the denominators together, factorising and simplifying where possible.

For division, multiply the first fraction by the reciprocal of the second fraction (flip the second fraction).

For addition or subtraction, find a common denominator by multiplying the denominators together.

Convert each fraction to this common denominator, combine the numerators over it, and simplify the result.

## Examiner tip

Never attempt to cancel individual terms from a numerator and denominator if they are linked by addition or subtraction signs. For example, in the expression xx2+5, you cannot cancel the x terms to get x+5. Terms can only be canceled when the numerator and denominator are fully factorised into products of factors.

## Quick check

To find a common denominator for two algebraic fractions, multiply their individual denominators together.

## Worked example — Example 1 (June 2022 P21 Q18)

Simplify the algebraic fraction completely: 2x2−5x−3x2−9.

Factorise the numerator. Recognize x . −9 as a difference of two squares: (x−3)(x+3).

Factorise the quadratic expression in the denominator: 2x2−5x−3=(2x+1)(x−3).

Rewrite the fraction using these factorised forms: (2x+1)(x−3)(x−3)(x+3).

Identify and cancel the common factor bracket (x−3) from both the top and bottom.

State the remaining terms as the final simplified fraction: 2x+1x+3.

## Worked example — Example 2 (Nov 2023 P42 Q7a)

Write as a single fraction in its simplest form: x+23−x−12.

Identify the common denominator by multiplying the two distinct denominator brackets together: (x+2)(x−1).

Adjust the numerators by cross-multiplying each by the opposite denominator: (x+2)(x−1)3(x−1)−(x+2)(x−1)2(x+2).

Combine the numerators over the shared common denominator: (x+2)(x−1)3(x−1)−2(x+2).

Expand the expressions in the numerator carefully: 3x−3−2x−4. Collect like terms in the numerator: (3x−2x)+(−3−4)=x−7. State the final combined fraction: (x+2)(x−1)x−7.
