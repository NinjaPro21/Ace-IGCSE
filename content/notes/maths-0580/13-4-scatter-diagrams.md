## Core idea

Scatter diagrams show whether two variables are related. A line of best fit models the trend so you can estimate $y$ and $x$ — only within the data range.

## Key formulas

$$
\text{Positive correlation: } y \text{ increases as } x \text{ increases}
$$

$$
\text{Negative correlation: } y \text{ decreases as } x \text{ increases}
$$

## Steps / method

**Draw a line of best fit**

Plot all points accurately.

Place a ruler through the cloud — roughly equal points above and below.

Draw one straight line; it does not have to pass through $(0,0)$.

**Read an estimate**

Find $x$ on the horizontal axis.

Go up to the line, then across to the $y$-axis.

## Examiner tip

Use the terms **positive correlation** or **negative correlation** — not “the line goes up”. Say **strong** or **weak / slight** for how close the points are to the line.

## Quick check

No clear pattern means **no correlation** — a line of best fit cannot be drawn reliably.

## Worked example — Example 1 (May 2021 P21 Q4)

Study hours ($x$ and $y$) show positive correlation. Explain in context.

As study hours increase, exam marks tend to increase — students who study more tend to score higher.

## Worked example — Example 2 (Nov 2022 P22 Q6)

Estimate $y$ when $x = 45$ if the line passes through $(20, 30)$ and $(50, 70)$.

Find $x = 45$ on the axis, read up to the line, then across to $y \approx 63.3$.
