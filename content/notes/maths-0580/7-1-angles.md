## Core idea

Angle geometry uses geometric rules to calculate unknown angles formed by intersecting lines, parallel lines, and polygons. These properties allow you to find unknown geometric angles without needing to measure them manually. Use these rules to solve multi-step geometry problems, verify if lines are parallel, and calculate the interior or exterior dimensions of regular polygons.

## Key formulas

$$
\text{Sum of Interior Angles in a Polygon:} \quad S_{\text{int}} = (n - 2) \times 180^\circ
$$
$$
\text{Regular Polygon Exterior Angle Rule:} \quad \text{Exterior Angle} = \frac{360^\circ}{n}
$$
$$
\text{Interior-Exterior Supplementary Link:} \quad \text{Interior Angle} + \text{Exterior Angle} = 180^\circ
$$

## Steps / method

Scan the geometric diagram to locate key structural features, such as parallel lines (marked with arrows), triangles, or multi-sided polygons.

Apply line rules first: look for vertically opposite angles (which are equal), angles on a straight line (which add to $180^\circ$), or angles at a point (which add to $360^\circ$).

Use parallel line rules next: look for Z-shapes (alternate angles match), F-shapes (corresponding angles match), or C-shapes (co-interior angles add to $180^\circ$).

For regular polygon problems, determine $n$ to calculate the total interior angle sum before finding unknown values.

## Examiner tip

When solving multi-step angle problems on Paper 2, you must write down the formal geometric name for the rules you use if the question asks you to "give reasons for your answers." Using informal terms like "Z-angles" or "F-angles" will lose you marks. Always use the correct mathematical terms: alternate angles, corresponding angles, or co-interior angles.

## Quick check

The sum of the exterior angles of any convex polygon, no matter how many sides it has, is always exactly $360^\circ$.

## Worked example — Example 1 (June 2022 P21 Q3)

A regular polygon has an exterior angle measuring $24^\circ$. Determine $n$ that this polygon possesses.

State the relationship between a regular polygon's exterior angles and its number of sides: $\text{Exterior Angle} = \frac{360^\circ}{n}$.

Substitute the given angle value into the formula: $24^\circ = \frac{360^\circ}{n}$.

Rearrange the equation to isolate $n$ when $2$. Compute the division to find the number of sides: $n = 15$.

## Worked example — Example 2 (Nov 2023 P42 Q4a)

In a multi-step geometric diagram, two parallel horizontal lines are cut by a sloping transversal line. Given that an interior angle on the lower parallel line is $115^\circ$, find the size of its corresponding angle and its co-interior angle on the upper parallel line.

Recall the corresponding angle rule: angles in matching positions on parallel lines are equal. Therefore, the corresponding angle is $115^\circ$.

Recall the co-interior angle rule: interior angles on the same side of the transversal line add up to $180^\circ$.

Set up the equation for the co-interior angle: $\text{Angle} + 115^\circ = 180^\circ$.

Subtract $115^\circ$ from $180^\circ$: $\text{Angle} = 180^\circ - 115^\circ = 65^\circ$.
