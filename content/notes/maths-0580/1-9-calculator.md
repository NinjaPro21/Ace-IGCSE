## Core idea

Calculator workflows focus on configuring and utilizing your electronic scientific calculator efficiently. This includes managing menu systems, using storage registers, and applying correct syntax operators during calculation entries. Master these settings to guarantee accuracy, preserve full numerical precision, and fulfill the structural formatting requirements of the IGCSE syllabus.

## Steps / method

Verify before every exam session that your calculator is operating in Degree Mode ($\text{D}$ or $\text{DEG}$) rather than Radian or Gradian mode.

Use the fraction button tool ($\frac{\Box}{\Box}$) to enter complex multi-level fractional expressions.

This ensures operations are performed in the correct order without requiring excess brackets.

Use the storage function keys ($\text{STO}$ and $\text{RCL}$) to save long, non-terminating intermediate decimal values.

Do not write down and manually re-enter truncated values mid-calculation.

Convert final values between improper fractions, mixed numbers, and decimal layouts using the standard display toggle key ($\text{S}\Leftrightarrow\text{D}$).

## Examiner tip

Unless a question specifies a different degree of accuracy, the official Cambridge instructions mandate that all non-exact numerical answers must be rounded to exactly three significant figures. Answers expressed in degrees must be rounded to exactly one decimal place. Rounding too early in the middle of a multi-step problem will cause accuracy errors that lose you marks on your final answer.

## Quick check

Always verify your calculator's mode setting at the start of an exam by evaluating $\sin(30)$; if the output does not equal exactly $0.5$, your calculator is set to the wrong angle mode.

## Worked example — Example 1 (June 2021 P21 Q1)

Calculate the value of $\frac{\sqrt{4.21 + 7.89}}{3.2 \times 1.45}$, writing down your full unrounded calculator display output.

Enter the fraction input mode on your scientific calculator.

Type the square root expression along with its inner terms carefully into the numerator field: $\sqrt{4.21 + 7.89}$.

Navigate down to the denominator field and enter the multiplication string: $3.2 \times 1.45$.

Press execute to compute the result, yielding the raw decimal string: $0.74972144$.

Transcribe this complete unrounded string onto your exam script exactly as displayed.

## Worked example — Example 2 (Nov 2023 P22 Q2)

Round your calculated value from Example 1 to satisfy standard Cambridge IGCSE syllabus accuracy instructions. Review the raw output value collected: $0.74972144$.

Recall the general precision rule: non-exact values must be rounded to $3$ significant figures.

Identify the first three significant digits starting from the left at the first non- zero digit: $7$, $4$, and $9$.

Check the fourth digit ($7$) to determine whether to round up or down. Since $7 \ge 5$, round up the third digit.

Rounding up $9$ shifts the preceding value, giving a final rounded result of $0.750$.
