## Core idea

Arithmetic establishes the foundational rules for managing basic numerical calculations using the four core operations: addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division. It mandates strict adherence to the Order of Operations (BIDMAS/BODMAS) to evaluate multi-step numerical calculations consistently. Use this framework whenever you need to evaluate mixed expressions containing brackets, powers, or nested fractions before attempting algebraic manipulations.

## Key formulas

$$
\text{Order of Operations:} \quad \text{Brackets} \rightarrow \text{Indices} \rightarrow \text{Division / Multiplication} \rightarrow \text{Addition / Subtraction}
$$

$$
\text{Fractional Operation Rule:} \quad \frac{a}{b} \div \frac{c}{d} = \frac{a}{b} \times \frac{d}{c} = \frac{ad}{bc}
$$

## Steps / method

Scan the numerical expression from left to right to identify and isolate expressions enclosed within brackets, simplifying inner terms first.

Evaluate any indices, powers, or roots present in the expression.

Perform multiplication and division processes sequentially as they appear from left to right, treating them with equal priority.

Execute final addition and subtraction operations from left to right to determine the final simplified value.

## Examiner tip

A classic Paper 2 trap involves failing to treat a long fraction bar as an implicit set of brackets. When evaluating an expression like $\frac{12 + 18}{2 \times 3}$, you must calculate the entire numerator and the entire denominator separately before dividing. Entering 12 + 18 ÷ 2 × 3 sequentially into a basic calculator without brackets will lead to an incorrect result due to standard operation priority rules.

## Quick check

If an calculation contains both a multiplication and a division running sequentially, evaluate them strictly from left to right rather than prioritizing multiplication over division.

## Worked example — Example 1 (May 2021 P2 Q3)

Evaluate $4 + 5 \times (3^2 - 4)$.

Handle the inner bracket terms first, beginning with the index: $3^2 - 4 = 9 - 4$. Complete the bracket evaluation: $9 - 4 = 5$.

Substitute the bracket value back into the primary expression: $4 + 5 \times 5$.

Apply operation priority by multiplying before adding: $5 \times 5 = 25$. Perform the final addition: $4 + 25 = 29$.

## Worked example — Example 2 (Nov 2023 P2 Q11)

Calculate $\frac{2}{3} + \frac{1}{4} \times \frac{2}{5}$.

Identify operations: addition and multiplication are present. Under BIDMAS rules, multiplication takes precedence.

Calculate the product of the fractions: $\frac{1}{4} \times \frac{2}{5} = \frac{1 \times 2}{4 \times 5} = \frac{2}{20}$.

Simplify the resulting fraction to its lowest terms: $\frac{2}{20} = \frac{1}{10}$.

Set up the addition with a common denominator for $3$ and $10$, which is $30$: $\frac{2 \times 10}{3 \times 10} + \frac{1 \times 3}{10 \times 3} = \frac{20}{30} + \frac{3}{30}$.

Combine numerators over the shared denominator: $\frac{20 + 3}{30} = \frac{23}{30}$.
