## Core idea

Linear equations are mathematical statements where an unknown variable has a maximum index power of 1. Solving a linear equation involves performing inverse operations symmetrically on both sides to isolate the variable. Use this process to find unknown values, interpret linear graphs, and solve word problems by converting real-world scenarios into equations.

## Key formulas

Standard Linear Form:ax+b=c Symmetric Balance Inversion Rule:If ax−b=c $\Rightarrow$ x=ac+b

Linear equations — keep the balance: do the same operation to both sides.

## Steps / method

Clear any fractions by multiplying every term in the equation by the lowest common denominator, and expand any brackets that are present.

Move all terms containing the unknown variable to one side of the equation using inverse operations, and move all constant numerical values to the opposite side.

Combine like terms on both sides to simplify the equation to the form ax=b.

Divide both sides of the equation by the variable's coefficient a to determine the exact value of x.

## Examiner tip

If an equation features the unknown variable on both sides, such as 3x+4=7x−8, clear the variable with the smaller coefficient first. Subtracting 3x from both sides gives 4=4x−8. This approach keeps the variable coefficient positive, avoiding errors with negative divisions later on.

## Quick check

Multiplying or dividing an entire equation by a negative number changes the sign of every single term on both sides.

## Worked example — Example 1 (June 2023 P21 Q11)

Solve the linear equation: 4(x−3)=7x+9. Expand the brackets on the left side: 4x−12=7x+9.

Subtract 4x from both sides to gather the variables on the right side: −12=3x+9.

Subtract 9 from both sides to isolate the variable term: −12−9=3x So −21=3x. Divide both sides by 3 to find the final x=3−21=−7.

## Worked example — Example 2 (Nov 2022 P42 Q3c)

Solve the fractional linear equation: 32x+5−4x−1=2.

Identify the common denominator for 3 and 4, which is 12. Multiply every term in the equation by 12 to clear the fractions: 12(32x+5)−12(4x−1)=12(2).

Simplify the multipliers against the denominators: 4(2x+5)−3(x−1)=24.

Expand both brackets carefully, keeping track of the negative sign on the second bracket: 8x+20−3x+3=24. Collect like terms on the left side: (8x−3x)+(20+3)=24

So 5x+23=24. Subtract 23 from both sides: 5x=1. Divide by 5 to find the final answer: x=51 (or 0.2).
