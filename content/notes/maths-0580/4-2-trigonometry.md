## Core idea

SOH CAH TOA links angles and side lengths in right-angled triangles. Use them to find missing sides or angles.

## Key formulas

$\sin\theta = \frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Hypotenuse}}$

$\cos\theta = \frac{\text{Adjacent}}{\text{Hypotenuse}}$

$\tan\theta = \frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Adjacent}}$

$\tan\theta = \frac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta}$

## Steps / method

Label **Opposite**, **Adjacent**, and **Hypotenuse** relative to angle $\theta$.

Pick the ratio that uses the two sides you know (SOH, CAH, or TOA). 3. **Find a side:** rearrange and calculate on your calculator.

 4. **Find an angle:** use $\sin^{-1}$, $\cos^{-1}$, or $\tan^{-1}$.

## Examiner tip

Set your calculator to **Degree mode (D)** before any trig calculation.

## Quick check

For an acute angle in a right triangle, $\sin\theta$. $\cos\theta. 0$ and $1$.

## Worked example — Example 1 (June 2022 P22 Q13)

A right triangle has hypotenuse $15\text{ cm}$ and angle $35^\circ$ and $35^\circ$.

Use sine (opp and hyp): $\sin 35^\circ = \frac{x}{15} \Rightarrow x = 15\sin 35^\circ \approx 8.60\text{ cm}$

## Worked example — Example 2 (Nov 2023 P41 Q2b)

A ladder $6\text{ m}$ long has its foot $1.8\text{ m}$ from a wall. Find the angle with the ground.

Use cosine (adj and hyp): $\cos\theta = \frac{1.8}{6} = 0.3 \Rightarrow \theta = \cos^{-1}(0.3) \approx 72.5^\circ$
