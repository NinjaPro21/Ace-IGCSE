## Core idea

Changing the subject of a formula involves rearranging its terms to isolate a target variable on one side of the equals sign, making it the subject. This process uses inverse operations in reverse order to undo operations applied to the target variable. Use this technique to adapt formulas for direct evaluation or to prepare equations for substitution.

## Key formulas

$$
\text{Isolating squares: } x^2 = y \Rightarrow x = \sqrt{y}
$$

$$
\text{Factoring the subject: } ax + bx = c \Rightarrow x = \frac{c}{a + b}
$$

## Steps / method

Clear any fractions by multiplying all terms in the formula by the denominator, and expand any brackets that contain the target variable.

Move all terms containing the target variable to one side of the equation, and move all other terms to the opposite side.

If the target variable appears in multiple terms, factorise the expression to extract the target variable as a single factor outside a set of brackets.

Undo any remaining operations, such as dividing by the bracket coefficient or taking a square root, to isolate the target variable completely.

## Examiner tip

When a formula contains the target variable in two different places, such as $y = \frac{x - 3}{x + 2}$ where you need to make $x$ the subject, you must multiply out the fraction and collect all terms containing $x$ on one side. Once gathered, factorise out $x$ to get $x(y - 1) = 3y + 2$. Trying to isolate $x$ without factorising first is a common pitfall on Paper 4.

## Quick check

To isolate a variable trapped inside a square root sign, raise the entire expression on the opposite side of the equation to the power of two.

## Worked example — Example 1 (June 2023 P22 Q14)

Rearrange the formula to make $x$ the subject: $A = 3x^2 - 5$.

Isolate the term containing $x$ by adding $5$ to both sides of the formula: $A + 5 = 3x^2$.

Divide both sides by $3$ to isolate the squared variable: $\frac{A + 5}{3} = x^2$.

Take the square root of both sides to isolate $x$ completely: $x = \sqrt{\frac{A + 5}{3}}$.

## Worked example — Example 2 (Nov 2022 P41 Q5c)

Make the variable $t$ the subject of the formula: $p = \frac{2t + 3}{t - 5}$.

Clear the fraction by multiplying both sides by the denominator bracket $(t - 5)$: $p(t - 5) = 2t + 3$.

Expand the bracket on the left side: $pt - 5p = 2t + 3$.

Move all terms containing $t$ to the left side by subtracting $2t$, and move the $-5p$ term to the right side by adding $5p$: $pt - 2t = 5p + 3$.

Factorise out the target variable $t$ on the left side: $t(p - 2) = 5p + 3$.

Divide by the bracket coefficient $(p - 2)$ to isolate $t$ completely: $t = \frac{5p + 3}{p - 2}$.
