## Core idea

Standard form (scientific notation) writes very large or very small numerical values as a product of a number and a power of ten. This notation streamlines calculations and clarifies precision tracking across scientific fields and global data frameworks. Use standard form whenever your calculations exceed normal display bounds or involve astronomical distances or microscopic measurements.

## Key formulas

$$
\text{Standard Form Format:} \quad A \times 10^n \quad \text{where} \quad 1 \le A < 10 \quad \text{and} \quad n \in \mathbb{Z}
$$

## Steps / method

Locate the position of the existing decimal point within the raw value.

Shift this decimal point until you create a number $A$. $1$ but strictly less than $10$.

Count the total number of places the decimal point shifted.

This count determines the integer value of the exponent power $n$.

Assign a positive exponent value if the original number was large ($\ge 10$) and the decimal moved left.

Assign a negative exponent value if the original number was small ($< 1$) and the decimal moved right.

## Examiner tip

When completing calculations in standard form on Paper 2, always check that your final value meets the required definition ($1 \le A < 10$). If a calculation yields an unsimplified result like $42.5 \times 10^{5}$, you will lose marks. You must adjust this value to $4.25 \times 10^{6}$ to present it in correct standard form.

## Quick check

A negative exponent value within a standard form expression means the original number is a decimal value strictly between $-1$ and $1$.

## Worked example — Example 1 (June 2022 P22 Q6)

Calculate $(4.5 \times 10^{7}) \times (2.0 \times 10^{-3})$, stating your final answer in standard form.

Group the numerical coefficient components and the powers of ten separately: $(4.5 \times 2.0) \times (10^{7} \times 10^{-3})$.

Compute the product of the numerical coefficients: $4.5 \times 2.0 = 9.0$.

Compute the product of the powers of ten by adding their exponents together: $10^{7 + (-3)} = 10^{4}$. Combine both components: $9.0 \times 10^{4}$.

Verify that the coefficient $9.0$ is within the required range ($1 \le 9.0 < 10$), confirming it is in correct standard form.

## Worked example — Example 2 (Nov 2021 P42 Q2a)

Find the value of $(1.2 \times 10^{4}) + (8.5 \times 10^{3})$, presenting the result in correct standard form format.

Notice that the exponents of the powers of ten differ, meaning you cannot add the coefficients directly.

Convert one of the values so that both terms share the same power of ten. Let's convert the second term to match $10^{4}$: $8.5 \times 10^{3} = 0.85 \times 10^{4}$.

Rewrite the addition expression with this shared base: $(1.2 \times 10^{4}) + (0.85 \times 10^{4})$.

Factor out the shared power of ten and add the coefficients: $(1.2 + 0.85) \times 10^{4} = 2.05 \times 10^{4}$.

Check that the coefficient $2.05$ fits the required definition, which it does.
