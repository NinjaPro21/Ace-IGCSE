## Core idea

Histograms display continuous data. When class widths differ, use **frequency density** on the vertical axis so bar **area** represents frequency.

## Key formulas

$$
\text{Frequency density} = \frac{\text{Frequency}}{\text{Class width}}
$$

$$
\text{Sector angle} = \frac{\text{Frequency}}{\text{Total}} \times 360^{\circ}
$$

## Steps / method

**Draw a histogram**

Find each class width.

Calculate frequency density: FD $= f \div \text{width}$.

Plot FD as bar height — check that bar area equals frequency.

**Find frequency from a bar**

$$
\text{Frequency} = \text{FD} \times \text{class width}
$$

## Examiner tip

Never plot raw frequency on the y-axis when class widths are unequal.

## Quick check

In a histogram, frequency equals the **area** of the bar, not just its height.

## Worked example — Example 1 (May 2021 P42 Q4a)

Interval $50 < w \le 60$, $f = 35$. Interval $60 < w \le 80$, $f = 50$.

Widths: 10 and 20. FD: $\frac{35}{10} = 3.5$ and $\frac{50}{20} = 2.5$.

## Worked example — Example 2 (Nov 2020 P41 Q5b)

Bar from 10 to 25, height FD $= 4.2$. Width $= 15$. Frequency $= 15 \times 4.2 = 63$.
