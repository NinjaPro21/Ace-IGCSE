## Core idea

Simplifying algebraic expressions involves expanding brackets using the distributive property and combining like terms. Like terms must share identical variable letters raised to exactly the same index powers. Use these expansion techniques to simplify expressions, rearrange formulas, and prepare complex equations for solving.

## Key formulas

$$
\text{Single bracket: } a(b + c) = ab + ac
$$

$$
\text{Double brackets: } (a + b)(c + d) = ac + ad + bc + bd
$$

$$
(a + b)^2 = a^2 + 2ab + b^2, \quad (a - b)^2 = a^2 - 2ab + b^2
$$

## Steps / method

Expand single brackets by multiplying the term outside the bracket by every term inside, tracking signs carefully.

Expand double brackets systematically using a method like FOIL (First, Outer, Inner, Last) or a grid diagram to ensure no term combinations are missed.

Group terms with identical variable bases and matching powers together, keeping their preceding operational signs attached.

Add or subtract the numerical coefficients of like terms to write the final expression in its simplest form.

## Examiner tip

A very common error occurs when expanding a negative term across brackets, such as $-3(x - 4)$. Students often forget to multiply the negative signs together, incorrectly writing $-3x - 12$. The correct expansion is $-3x + 12$. Always check the sign of every single product.

## Quick check

The terms $x^2$ and $x$ cannot be added together into a single term because they have different index powers.

## Worked example — Example 1 (June 2022 P22 Q8)

Expand the brackets and simplify: $5(2x - 3) - 2(x - 7)$.

Expand the first single bracket: $5 \times 2x - 5 \times 3 = 10x - 15$.

Expand the second single bracket, multiplying carefully by $-2$: $-2 \times x - 2 \times (-7) = -2x + 14$.

Combine both parts into a single expression: $10x - 15 - 2x + 14$.

Group and collect like terms: $(10x - 2x) + (-15 + 14)$.

Simplify the coefficients to find the final answer: $8x - 1$.

## Worked example — Example 2 (Nov 2023 P21 Q14)

Expand and simplify fully: $(3x - 4)(2x + 5)$.

Multiply the First terms of both brackets: $3x \times 2x = 6x^2$.

Multiply the Outer terms: $3x \times 5 = 15x$.

Multiply the Inner terms: $-4 \times 2x = -8x$.

Multiply the Last terms: $-4 \times 5 = -20$.

Combine all four terms: $6x^2 + 15x - 8x - 20$.

Collect the middle like terms $(15x - 8x)$ to find the final simplified answer: $6x^2 + 7x - 20$.
