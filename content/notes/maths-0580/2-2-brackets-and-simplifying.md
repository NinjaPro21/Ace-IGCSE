## Core idea

Simplifying algebraic expressions involves expanding brackets using the distributive property and combining like terms. Like terms must share identical variable letters raised to exactly the same index powers. Use these expansion techniques to simplify expressions, rearrange formulas, and prepare complex equations for solving.

## Steps / method

Expand single brackets by multiplying the term outside the bracket by every term inside, tracking signs carefully.

Expand double brackets systematically using a method like FOIL (First, Outer, Inner, Last) or a grid diagram to ensure no term combinations are missed.

Group terms with identical variable bases and matching powers together, keeping their preceding operational signs attached.

Add or subtract the numerical coefficients of like terms to write the final expression in its simplest form.

## Examiner tip

A very common error occurs when expanding a negative term across brackets, such as вҲ’3(xвҲ’4). Students often forget to multiply the negative signs together, incorrectly writing вҲ’3xвҲ’12. The correct expansion is вҲ’3x+12. Always check the sign of every single product.
