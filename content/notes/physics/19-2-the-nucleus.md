## Core idea

The nucleus is a tiny, dense core containing nearly all of an atom's mass. Nuclide notation and the link between proton number and nucleon number are essential for nuclear physics and radioactivity.

## Key definitions

- **Proton**: Positively charged particle in the nucleus (relative charge $+1$).
- **Neutron**: Neutral particle in the nucleus (relative charge $0$).
- **Nucleon**: A proton or neutron in the nucleus.
- **Proton number ($Z$)**: Number of protons (atomic number).
- **Nucleon number ($A$)**: Total protons + neutrons (mass number).
- **Isotopes**: Same element (same $Z$) but different numbers of neutrons.
- **Nuclide**: A specific nucleus written as ${}^A_Z X$.

## Key formulas

**Number of neutrons**

$$
\text{Number of neutrons} = A - Z
$$
**Relative charge on nucleus**

$$
\text{Relative charge} = +Z
$$

**Isotope comparison**: Isotopes (e.g. carbon-12 vs carbon-14) have the same $Z$ but different neutron counts.

## Steps / method

1. **Identify** the ${}^A_Z X$ notation in the question.
2. **Protons** = $Z$ (bottom number).
3. **Electrons** = $Z$ for a neutral atom.
4. **Neutrons** = $A - Z$.
5. **Isotopes** — same $Z$, different $A$.

## Common mistakes

- Including electrons in the nucleon number — only protons and neutrons are in the nucleus.
- Swapping $A$ (top) and $Z$ (bottom).
- Thinking different neutron counts make a different element — only $Z$ defines the element.

## Examiner tip

Check whether the question says "neutral atom" — electrons = protons only for neutral atoms. Isotopes have identical chemical properties (same $Z$ and electrons) but different physical properties (different mass).

## Worked example — Basic nucleus composition

Question: An atom of iodine is ${}^{131}_{53}\text{I}$. How many protons and neutrons are in the nucleus?

$Z = 53$ protons

$A = 131$, so neutrons $= 131 - 53 = 78$

## Worked example — Identifying isotopes

Question: Atom X has 17 protons and 18 neutrons. Atom Y has 17 protons and 20 neutrons. Give the nuclide notation for X and explain the relationship.

For X: $Z = 17$, $A = 17 + 18 = 35$ → ${}^{35}_{17}\text{X}$

Both have 17 protons → same element. Different neutron counts → **isotopes**.
