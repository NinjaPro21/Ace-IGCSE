## Core idea

In a series circuit, components are connected one after another in a single loop. There is only one path for charge to flow.

## Combined e.m.f.

When cells are connected in series, their e.m.f.s add. Two 1.5 V cells in series give 3.0 V combined.

## Current in a series circuit

The current is the same at every point. Conservation of charge means no charge escapes or enters the loop.

## Potential difference (p.d.)

The total p.d. across all components equals the sum of individual p.d.s:

$$
V_E = V_1 + V_2 + \cdots + V_n
$$

The e.m.f. ($E$) from the source equals the total p.d. ($V_E$) converted by the components.

## Combined resistance

For resistors in series:

$$
R = R_1 + R_2 + \cdots + R_n
$$

The same current $I$ passes through each resistor, so total work per unit charge is $IR_1 + IR_2 + \cdots$.

## Key characteristics

- The component with larger resistance has a greater p.d. across it.
- If one component fails and breaks the loop, the entire circuit becomes open and all components stop working.
