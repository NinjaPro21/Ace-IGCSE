## Core idea

An a.c. generator transforms mechanical energy into electrical energy using electromagnetic induction.

## Construction and operation

- **Armature**: Rectangular coil on an axle, rotating between permanent magnet poles.
- **Slip rings**: Rotate with the axle; transfer induced current to the external circuit.
- **Carbon brushes**: Press against slip rings for continuous electrical contact.
- **Process**: Rotating the coil changes magnetic flux, inducing an e.m.f. that powers a load.

## Fleming's right-hand rule

For induced current in a conductor moving through a magnetic field:

- **Thumb**: Motion of the wire
- **Forefinger**: Magnetic field (N → S)
- **Second finger**: Induced current

## Voltage–time relationship

- **Maximum voltage**: Coil plane parallel to the field — wires cut field lines at the greatest rate.
- **Zero voltage**: Coil plane perpendicular to the field — rate of change of flux is zero.

## Increasing induced e.m.f.

1. More turns in the coil
2. Stronger magnets
3. Faster rotation speed
4. Soft iron core to strengthen flux linkage

## Bicycle dynamo design

Magnets rotate while the coil stays fixed — no carbon brushes (more durable), less overheating, more compact.
