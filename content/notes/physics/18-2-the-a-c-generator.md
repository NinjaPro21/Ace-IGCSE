## Core idea

An alternating current (a.c.) generator is a device that transforms mechanical energy into electrical energy using the principle of electromagnetic induction.

Basic Construction and Operation

A simple a.c. generator consists of several key parts:

Armature: A rectangular coil of wire mounted on an axle that rotates between the poles of permanent magnets.

Slip Rings: These rotate with the axle and ensure the induced current is transferred to the external circuit.

Carbon Brushes: These press lightly against the slip rings to maintain a constant electrical connection as the axle turns.

Process: Turning the handle rotates the coil in the magnetic field, creating a change in magnetic flux. This change induces an e.m.f. and a current that powers an electrical load, such as a lamp.

Fleming’s Right-Hand Rule

This rule is used to determine the direction of the induced current in a conductor moving through a magnetic field:

Thumb: Points in the direction of the motion (force) of the wire.

Forefinger: Points in the direction of the magnetic field (North to South).

Second Finger: Points in the direction of the induced current.

Voltage–Time Relationship

The induced e.m.f. (output voltage) changes its magnitude and direction as the coil rotates:

Maximum Voltage: Occurs when the plane of the coil is parallel to the magnetic field. At this position, the wire cuts across the magnetic field lines at the greatest rate.

Zero Voltage: Occurs when the plane of the coil is perpendicular to the magnetic field. At this position, the rate of change of magnetic flux is zero.

Increasing the Induced e.m.f.

To produce a larger output voltage, you can: Increase the number of turns in the coil.

Use stronger permanent magnets.

Increase the speed (frequency) of rotation.

Wind the coil around a soft iron core to strengthen the magnetic flux linkage.

Practical Design: The Bicycle Dynamo

In many practical applications, such as a bicycle dynamo, a fixed coil design is used. In this setup, the magnets rotate while the coil remains stationary.

Advantages of this design include:

Durability: It does not require carbon brushes, which wear out and need frequent replacement.

Reliability: It is less likely to overheat because there are no slip rings to create frictional resistance.

Size: The design is more compact than a traditional simple generator.
