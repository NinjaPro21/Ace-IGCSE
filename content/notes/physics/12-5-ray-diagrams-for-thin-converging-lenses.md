## Core idea

The position and nature of an image formed by a thin converging lens depends on the object distance relative to the focal length. Three predictable rays allow us to construct ray diagrams and locate images precisely.

## Key definitions

- **Object distance ($u$)**: Distance of the object from the optical centre.
- **Image distance ($v$)**: Distance of the image from the optical centre.
- **Real image**: Formed where rays actually meet; can be projected on a screen.
- **Virtual image**: Formed where rays appear to meet; cannot be projected.

## Predictable rays

1. Through optical centre ($C$) — travels straight.
2. Parallel to principal axis → refracts through $F$ on the opposite side.
3. Through $F$ before the lens → refracts parallel to the principal axis.

## Image characteristics

| Object distance | Image type | Orientation | Size | Application |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| $u = \infty$ | Real | Inverted | Diminished | Telescope objective |
| $u > 2f$ | Real | Inverted | Diminished | Camera, eye |
| $u = 2f$ | Real | Inverted | Same size | Photocopier |
| $f < u < 2f$ | Real | Inverted | Magnified | Projector |
| $u = f$ | — | — | — | Rays parallel (no image) |
| $u < f$ | Virtual | Upright | Magnified | Magnifying glass |

**Key rule**: $u > f$ → real, inverted, opposite side; $u \leq f$ → virtual, upright, same side.

Use the **interactive explorer** above to switch between object-distance scenarios and see the matching ray diagram.

## Practical applications

- **Magnifying glass**: Object at $u < f$ → virtual, upright, magnified image.
- **Long sight**: Converging lens helps eye focus nearby objects.
- **Short sight**: Diverging lens spreads rays before they enter the eye.
