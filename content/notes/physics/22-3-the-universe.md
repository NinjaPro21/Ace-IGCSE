## Core idea

2$^2.3 The Universe

The Universe is so vast that it contains billions of galaxies, each housing billions of stars. By observing distant light, scientists have discovered that the Universe is not static; it is expanding. Understanding redshift and the Hubble constant allows us to look back in time to the very beginning—the Big Bang—providing a fundamental perspective on our place in the cosmos.

## Key definitions

- **Galaxy**: A group of many billions of stars.
- **Milky Way**: The spiral galaxy containing our Sun, with an approximate diameter of $100000$ light-years.
- **Redshift**: The increase in the observed wavelength of electromagnetic radiation emitted from stars and galaxies moving away from Earth.
- **Big Bang Theory**: The theory that the Universe was created from a single point by expansion approximately $14$ billion years ago.
- **Cosmic Microwave Background Radiation (CMBR)**: Radiation produced shortly after the Universe was formed that has expanded into the microwave region of the EM spectrum.
- **Hubble Constant ($H_0$)**: The ratio of the speed ($v$) at which a galaxy is moving away from Earth to its distance ($d$) from Earth.
- **Light-year**: The distance light travels in a vacuum in one year, equal to $9.5 \times $10^{15}$
m.

## Steps / method

Exam Method for Analyzing Cosmological Expansion:

Identify Redshift: Confirm the observed wavelength has increased compared to the source. Determine Recession Speed ($v$): Use spectral line shifts to calculate how fast the galaxy moves away. Measure Distance ($d$): Use the brightness of objects like white dwarf supernovae to estimate distance. Apply Hubble's Law: Calculate $H_0$ using $H_0 = v/d$.

Estimate Age: Use $1/H_0$ to determine the time since the Big Bang.

## Common mistakes

Light-year as Time: Treating a light-year as a unit of time because it contains the word "year"; it is a unit of distance.

Expansion Origin: Thinking galaxies expand outward into empty space; actually, the space between galaxies is what expands.

Redshift Meaning: Assuming redshift means the galaxy looks red; it actually means its light's wavelength has increased.

## Examiner tip

Evidence Support: If asked for evidence of the Big Bang, mention both redshift of distant galaxies and CMBR.

SI Units: Ensure distances are converted to metres and time to seconds when using the Hubble constant value $2.2 \times $10^{-18}$\ s^{-1}$.
