## Core idea

Just as a magnetic field exists around a magnet, an electric field exists around every electric charge. It is an invisible region where other charges experience a force. Electric field patterns help predict how charged particles move in devices such as cathode-ray tubes and particle accelerators.

## Key definitions

- **Electric field**: A region of space in which an electric charge experiences a force.
- **Direction of electric field**: The direction of the force on a positive charge at that point.
- **Uniform field**: A field where the electric field lines are parallel and equally spaced, so field strength is the same at all points.

## Electric field patterns

Electric fields are shown using field lines with arrowheads indicating the force on a positive test charge.

**Point charges**

- **Positive point charge (+Q)**: Field lines radiate outward from the charge.
- **Negative point charge (−Q)**: Field lines point radially inward towards the charge.

**Charged conducting spheres**

- **External field**: Outside the sphere, the pattern is the same as for a point charge at the centre.
- **Internal field**: There are no electric field lines inside a charged conducting sphere.

**Parallel conducting plates**

- When two parallel plates are oppositely charged, field lines start on the positive plate and end on the negative plate.
- Near the middle of the plates, the lines are parallel — a uniform electric field.

## Steps / method

1. **Identify the source** — determine whether it is positive or negative.
2. **Draw direction** — lines point away from positive sources and towards negative sources.
3. **Show strength** — draw lines closer together where the force is stronger (near the charge).
4. **Check uniformity** — for parallel plates, keep central lines straight, parallel, and evenly spaced.

## Common mistakes

- **Direction error**: Drawing field lines pointing towards a positive charge. They show the force on a positive test charge, which would be repelled.
- **Crossing lines**: Electric field lines never cross.
- **Internal fields**: Drawing field lines inside a solid conducting sphere — the field exists only outside.
