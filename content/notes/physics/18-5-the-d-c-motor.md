## Core idea

A d.c. motor converts electrical energy into mechanical energy using the turning effect on a current-carrying coil in a magnetic field.

## Structure

- **Armature**: Rectangular coil (ABCD) on a rotating axle
- **Permanent magnets**: Provide a constant magnetic field
- **Split-ring commutator**: Connected to coil ends, rotates with the coil
- **Carbon brushes**: Maintain electrical contact with the external circuit
- **Rheostat**: Controls current into the motor

## Principle of operation

1. Current through the coil creates forces on opposite sides (Fleming's left-hand rule) — one up, one down.
2. Opposing forces create a turning effect (moment) → coil rotates.
3. The **commutator** reverses current direction every half revolution so forces always drive rotation the same way.
4. At the vertical position, brushes briefly lose contact; momentum carries the coil past this point.

## Increasing the turning effect

1. Insert a soft iron core
2. More turns in the coil
3. Increase the current (rheostat)
4. Stronger permanent magnets

## Practical applications

Battery toys, DVD players, hard disk drives, drone motors.
