## Core idea

Electric circuits transfer energy from a power source to components. As current flows, electrical energy is dissipated — converted into heat, light, or other forms — and transferred to the surroundings.

## Electrical power ($P$)

Power is the work done or energy transferred per unit time.

- **SI unit**: The watt (W), where $1\,\text{W} = 1\,\text{J/s}$.
- **Formula**:

$$
P = IV
$$

Where $P$ is power (W), $I$ is current (A), and $V$ is potential difference (V).

## Electrical energy ($E$)

The total energy transferred depends on power and time.

- **SI unit**: The joule (J).
- **Formula**:

$$
E = Pt = IVt
$$

Where $E$ is energy (J) and $t$ is time (s).

## Calculating the cost of electricity

Electricity companies use the kilowatt-hour (kWh) because the joule is very small for household billing.

- **Definition**: A kWh is the energy used by a $1\,\text{kW}$ appliance switched on for one hour.
- **Cost**:

$$
\text{Total cost} = \text{Energy used (kWh)} \times \text{Tariff (price per kWh)}
$$

## Worked example — Calculating energy and cost

Question: An electric kettle rated at $3\,\text{kW}$ is used for 20 minutes. A $100\,\text{W}$ light bulb is used for 5 hours. If the tariff is 27 cents per kWh, calculate the total cost.

Kettle: $E_1 = 3 \times \frac{20}{60} = 1\,\text{kWh}$

Bulb: $E_2 = 0.1 \times 5 = 0.5\,\text{kWh}$

Total energy $= 1 + 0.5 = 1.5\,\text{kWh}$

Total cost $= 1.5 \times 27 = 40.5$ cents (approximately $0.41)$

Which formula relates electrical power to current and voltage?
