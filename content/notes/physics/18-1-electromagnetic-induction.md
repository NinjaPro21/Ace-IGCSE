## Core idea

Electromagnetic induction produces an induced e.m.f. in a conductor when the magnetic flux through it changes. Faraday (1831) showed that a changing magnetic field can produce an electric current — the reverse of a current creating a magnetic field.

Relative motion between a magnet and a coil is essential: no motion means no induced e.m.f.

## Graphs & diagrams

**Core experiment — magnet and solenoid**

- **Moving IN**: N pole approaches → flux lines enter the coil → induced current clockwise (viewed from the right) → **centre-zero galvanometer** needle deflects **right**.
- **Stationary INSIDE**: Magnet at rest inside the coil → no flux change → **I = 0** → needle at **zero**.
- **Pulling OUT**: N pole withdrawn → flux decreases → induced current anticlockwise → needle deflects **left**.

## Key formulas

**Faraday's law**

$$
\text{Induced e.m.f.} \propto \text{rate of cutting of magnetic flux}
$$

The faster the magnetic flux through a coil changes, the larger the induced e.m.f.

**Lenz's law**

The induced current opposes the change that caused it.

- Push N pole into coil → induced N pole at that end (repulsion).
- Pull N pole out → induced S pole at that end (attraction).

## Factors increasing induced e.m.f.

- **Speed**: Move the magnet or coil faster — more flux lines are cut per second.
- **Strength**: Use a stronger permanent magnet — more flux lines enter the coil.
- **Turns**: Increase the number of turns on the solenoid — more conductors cut the flux.

## Faraday's experiment

A solenoid connected to a **centre-zero galvanometer** and a bar magnet:

- **Magnet moved in**: Needle deflects one way — induced current flows.
- **Magnet withdrawn**: Needle deflects the opposite way.
- **Magnet stationary**: No deflection — no induced e.m.f.

**Conclusion**: Relative movement between conductor and magnetic field is required.

## Conservation of energy

Lenz's law follows conservation of energy — without opposition, the magnet would be pulled in faster without external work, creating energy from nothing.
