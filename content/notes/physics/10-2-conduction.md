## Core idea

Conduction is the process of thermal energy transfer through a solid from a region of higher temperature to a region of lower temperature. This transfer occurs without any bulk movement of the material itself.

## Key definitions

- **Conduction**: The transfer of thermal energy through a medium (typically solids) without the bulk movement of the medium itself.
- **Thermal Conductors**: Materials that can transfer thermal energy quickly (e.g., metals).
- **Thermal Insulators**: Materials that transfer thermal energy slowly (e.g., non-metals like wood, plastic, and glass).
- **Lattice Vibration**: The vibration of particles (atoms or molecules) about fixed positions in a solid.
- **Free Electrons**: Electrons not firmly attached to any one atom that can move randomly through a metal; their presence significantly increases conduction speed.
Mechanism of Conduction
The kinetic particle model explains how conduction works differently in non-metals and metals: In Non-metals
- **Thermal transfer occurs solely via lattice vibrations**: Particles at the heated (hotter) end vibrate more vigorously about their fixed positions.
These particles collide with their less energetic neighbours, transferring kinetic energy to them.
Eventually, the particles at the cooler end also vibrate vigorously, making that end hot.
In Metals
Thermal transfer is much faster because it involves both lattice vibrations and free electron diffusion: Free electrons at the heated end absorb thermal energy and gain kinetic energy.
These electrons move at high speeds to the cooler regions of the metal.
As they move, they collide with atoms in the cooler parts, transferring kinetic energy and heating the cooler end much more rapidly than vibration alone could.
Conduction in Liquids and Gases
Liquids and gases are generally bad thermal conductors. Because their particles are spaced much further apart than in solids, collisions between particles are less frequent. This means the transfer of kinetic energy from fast-moving particles to their neighbours is very slow.
- **Experiment**: Water can be shown to be a bad conductor by heating a boiling tube of water at the top. The water at the top will boil while an ice cube held at the bottom remains frozen, proving that conduction downward is extremely slow.
