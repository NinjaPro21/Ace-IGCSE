## Core idea

Radiation is the transfer of thermal energy as invisible infrared waves. Unlike conduction and convection, radiation does not need a medium and is the only method by which thermal energy can travel through a vacuum — such as from the Sun to Earth.

## Key definitions

- **Thermal radiation**: Invisible infrared radiation that carries thermal energy.
- **Absorption**: Taking in infrared radiation, which increases an object's temperature.
- **Emission**: Giving out infrared radiation, which decreases an object's temperature.
- **Thermal equilibrium (radiation)**: When rates of emission and absorption are equal — no net temperature change.

## Surface properties

| Surface type | Absorption | Emission |
|--------------|------------|----------|
| **Dull black** | Best absorber | Best emitter |
| **Shiny silver** | Worst absorber (good reflector) | Worst emitter |

## Factors affecting radiation rate

1. **Surface colour and texture**: Dull black surfaces emit and absorb radiation faster than shiny silver surfaces.
2. **Surface temperature**: Higher temperature relative to surroundings → higher emission rate.
3. **Surface area**: Larger surface area → faster emission or absorption (same mass and material).

## Investigating emission and absorption

**Emission experiment**

1. Fill one dull black tin and one shiny silver tin with boiling water.
2. Record temperature over time with sensors.
3. Result: the dull black tin cools faster → better emitter.

**Absorption experiment**

1. Wrap one sensor in dull black foil and another in shiny silver foil.
2. Place both near a heat lamp.
3. Result: the black foil heats up faster → better absorber.

## Steps / method

**Explaining radiation transfer:**

1. State that all objects emit infrared radiation.
2. Explain that hotter objects emit more radiation than they absorb (net energy loss).
3. For the Sun: energy travels as electromagnetic radiation through the vacuum of space.
4. For surfaces: mention dull black = good absorber/emitter; shiny silver = poor absorber/emitter.

## Common mistakes

**Visible light vs infrared**: Thermal radiation is infrared — not the visible light we see from the Sun (though the Sun emits both).

**Shiny surfaces absorb well**: Shiny silver surfaces are **poor** absorbers and **good** reflectors.

**Radiation needs air**: Radiation works through a vacuum — it does not need particles.

## Examiner tip

In surface comparison questions, always pair absorption with emission: dull black is best at **both**. Space blankets work by being shiny (poor emitter + reflects body heat back).

## Quick check

Why can thermal energy from the Sun reach Earth despite the vacuum of space?

Which surface is a better emitter of infrared radiation: shiny silver or dull black?

If an object is at the same temperature as its surroundings, how do its rates of emission and absorption compare?

## Worked example — Comparing Surfaces

Question: Two identical cans of hot water — one painted dull black, one painted shiny silver — are left to cool in the same room. Which cools faster and why?

1. **Identify the process**: Both cans lose thermal energy mainly by radiation (and some convection from the air).

2. **Compare surfaces**: Dull black is a better emitter of infrared radiation than shiny silver.

3. **Conclusion**: The dull black can emits radiation faster and cools down more quickly.

**Final answer:** The dull black can cools faster because dull black surfaces are better emitters of infrared radiation.
