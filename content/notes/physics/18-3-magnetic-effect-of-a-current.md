## Core idea

In 1820, Oersted discovered that an electric current produces a magnetic field around the wire — the link between electricity and magnetism (electromagnetism).

## Magnetic field around a straight wire

- **Pattern**: Concentric circles centred on the wire.
- **Strength**: Strongest near the wire; increases with current.
- **Direction**: Reverses when current direction reverses.

**Right-hand grip rule (straight wire)**

1. Grip the wire with your right hand.
2. Thumb points in the direction of conventional current.
3. Curled fingers show the field direction (clockwise or anticlockwise).

## Magnetic field around a solenoid

A long coil of wire produces a field like a bar magnet — N pole at one end, S at the other. Inside, field lines are parallel and uniform.

**Strengthen the field by:**

1. Increasing the current
2. Increasing turns per unit length
3. Inserting a soft iron core

**Right-hand grip rule (solenoid)** — curl fingers in the direction of current; thumb points to the N pole.

## Applications

**Electrical relay** — a low-current circuit switches a high-current circuit via an electromagnet attracting an iron lever. Allows safe remote control of high-power equipment.

**Moving-coil loudspeaker** — a coil in a permanent magnet's field carries a.c.; rapid attraction and repulsion vibrates the diaphragm to produce sound.
