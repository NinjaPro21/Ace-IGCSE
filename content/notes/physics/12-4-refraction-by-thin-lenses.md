## Core idea

A lens is a piece of clear glass or plastic with curved surfaces that is used to focus or spread light. Lenses are foundational components in many optical instruments, including cameras, projectors, and spectacles. A lens can be thought of as a collection of small prisms; because the surfaces are curved, parallel rays strike different parts of the lens at different angles, causing them to refract and either converge or diverge.

## Key definitions

- **Principal Axis**: The horizontal line passing through the optical centre of the lens, perpendicular to the vertical plane of the lens.
- **Optical Centre ($C$)**: The midpoint between the surfaces of the lens on its principal axis; light rays passing through this point are not refracted.
- **Principal Focus / Focal Point ($F$)**: The point on the principal axis where all rays parallel to the axis converge after passing through the lens. Every lens has two focal points, one on each side.
- **Focal Length ($f$)**: The distance between the optical centre ($C$) and the principal focus ($F$).
- **Focal Plane**: The plane passing through the focal point ($F$) that is perpendicular to the principal axis.
Types of Lenses
- **Lenses are categorized by their shape and their effect on light rays**: - **Converging Lens**: This lens is thicker in the centre than at the edges. It causes parallel light rays to head towards a single point (converge).
- **Diverging Lens**: This lens is thinner in the centre than at the edges. It causes parallel light rays to spread out (diverge) from a point.
Determining Focal Length
You can estimate the focal length of a converging lens (like a magnifying glass) using the Sun: Hold the magnifying glass above a piece of paper outdoors under the Sun.
Adjust the distance between the lens and the paper until the Sun's rays are focused into a single, small bright spot.
The distance between the lens and the paper at this point is the focal length. Note: Care must be taken as the intense heat at the focal point can burn the paper.

## Worked example — — Lens Properties

- **Question**: How does the refraction of light differ at the outermost part of a lens compared to the middle? Solution: Because of the lens's curvature, the angle of refraction is largest at the outermost part of the lens. In contrast, light passing through the exact middle (the optical centre) does not undergo any refraction and continues in a straight line.
