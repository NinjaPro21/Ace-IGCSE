## Core idea

Refraction is the bending of light as it passes from one optical medium to another. This occurs because light travels at different speeds in different transparent materials. For example, light travels at $3.0 \times 10^8\ \text{m/s}$ in air but slows to approximately $2.0 \times 10^8\ \text{m/s}$ in glass. This change in speed at the boundary causes the light to change direction.

## Key definitions

- **Refraction**: The bending of light as it passes from one optical medium to another.
- **Normal**: The imaginary line perpendicular to the refracting surface at the point of incidence.
- **Angle of incidence ($i$)**: The angle between the incident ray and the normal.
- **Angle of refraction ($r$)**: The angle between the refracted ray and the normal.
- **Refractive index ($n$)**: The ratio of the speed of light in a vacuum to the speed of light in the medium.

## Key formulas

**Snell's law**

$n = \frac{\sin i}{\sin r}$

For two given media, the ratio of sines is constant.

**Speed and refractive index**

$n = \frac{c}{v}$

where $c$ is the speed of light in a vacuum and $v$ is the speed in the medium.

**Real and apparent depth**

$n = \frac{\text{real depth}}{\text{apparent depth}}$

Used when an object appears shallower when viewed through water or glass.

## Refraction rules

- **Toward the normal**: Entering an optically denser medium at an angle → $r < i$.
- **Away from the normal**: Entering a less dense medium at an angle → $r > i$.
- **Along the normal**: At $0^\circ$ to the normal, no bending (speed still changes).
- **Reversibility**: Reversing the ray follows the same path.

## Daily phenomena

- **Bent pencil**: Light from underwater bends away from the normal entering air, so the pencil looks bent.
- **Shallow pool**: Rays from the bottom bend at the surface, making the floor appear closer.

## Worked example — Speed of light in glass

Question: Calculate the speed of light in crown glass ($n = 1.5$). Speed of light in vacuum $c = 3.0 \times 10^{8}\ \text{m/s}$.

1. Rearrange: $v = c/n$.
2. Substitute: $v = (3.0 \times 10^{8}) / 1.5 = 2.0 \times 10^{8}\ \text{m/s}$.

**Final answer:** $2.0 \times 10^{8}\ \text{m/s}$.

## Common mistakes

- **Density confusion**: Optical density ($n$) is not the same as mass density.
- **Snell's law**: Use $\sin i$ and $\sin r$, not the angles themselves.
